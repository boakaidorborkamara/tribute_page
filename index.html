Life & Legacy

Malcolm X was a leader well known in the black community specifically in the US. He was a brave one defending civil rights and equity for black and white Americans.

QUOTE: “Any time you beg another man to set you free, you will never be free. Freedom is something that you have to do for yourselves .”

1941

Malcolm started his path as a criminal after he moved to Boston to live with his half-sister, Ella.

Malcolm was sentenced for some crimes he commited and was sent to prison where he decided to get educated.

1947

Malcolm met someone in prison, which in turn changed Malcolm's mindset and was a reason for him to convert to Islam. Hence he changed his name to Malcolm X.

1953

Malcolm was under investigation by the FBI due to his suspicious activity.
